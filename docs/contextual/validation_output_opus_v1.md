It teaches the upanisā (vital-condition) cascade: ethics is the vital condition for non-regret, which is for joy, rapture, tranquility, bliss, right immersion, true knowledge and vision, disillusionment and dispassion, and finally the knowledge and vision of freedom. This fragment is the positive form of the cascade, illustrated by the simile of a tree complete with branches and foliage whose shoots, bark, softwood, and heartwood grow to fullness.
